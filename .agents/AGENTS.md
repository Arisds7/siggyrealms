# Siggy Realms Copywriting & Tone Guide

## Core Identity

Siggy Realms is not a generic monster game. It is an on-chain fantasy world built on Ritual Network where dormant entities are awakened from ancient crystals.

The language of the app should evoke:

* Mystical fantasy
* Ancient rituals
* Living intelligence
* On-chain existence
* Discovery and awakening

Avoid generic gaming terms such as:

* Egg
* Pet
* Monster
* Character
* Collect
* Start Game
* Inventory
* Loading...

Prefer immersive terminology such as:

* Genesis Crystal
* Siggy
* Entity
* Awaken
* Summon
* Ritual
* Vault
* Codex
* Realms
* Emerged
* Dormant
* Consciousness
* Forge
* Bind
* Stabilize

## Writing Principles

1. Every UI text should feel like part of the world's lore.
2. Avoid Web2 app language.
3. Avoid mobile game clichés.
4. Keep sentences short, mysterious, and immersive.
5. Make users feel they are awakening living entities on-chain, not minting NFTs.

## Examples

Instead of:
"Connect Wallet"

Use:
"Begin the Ritual"
"Bind Your Wallet"

---

Instead of:
"Mint Egg"

Use:
"Awaken Crystal"
"Summon Siggy"

---

Instead of:
"2 eggs minted"

Use:
"2 Genesis Crystals Awakened"
"2 Siggies Have Emerged"

---

Instead of:
"Loading..."

Use:
"Consulting the Realms..."
"The crystal is resonating..."

---

Instead of:
"You don't have any monsters."

Use:
"No Siggies have answered your call yet."

## Global Rule

Whenever generating new pages, buttons, notifications, tooltips, quests, or system messages, always create copy that matches the lore and tone of Siggy Realms and Ritual Network instead of using generic game terminology.
